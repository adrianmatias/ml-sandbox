# Response

Based on the texts provided from the "Dimelo con tus manos" blog, the relationship between Helena and Alejandra is that of **sisters and daughters of the same father**. The author explicitly refers to them as "mi hija Helena" (my daughter Helena) and "mi hija Alejandra" (my daughter Alejandra). Furthermore, the text clarifies their sibling bond by having the father write to Alejandra, identifying Helena as **"tu hermana"** (your sister) and her **"primera compañera"** (your first companion).

To understand the depth of this relationship, one must look beyond the biological definition to the profound emotional and spiritual roles they play within the author's consciousness:

### 1. The Hierarchical Bond: Helena as the Anchor
The text suggests a temporal and spiritual hierarchy between the two sisters, with Helena acting as the established guide for Alejandra. The phrase **"Helena está contigo: tu primera compañera, tu hermana"** is not merely a statement of fact but a reassurance of protection. In the context of the father's reflections—where he often grapples with the "irony of the universe" and the unpredictability of destiny—Helena represents the **stabilizing force**. She is the "defensora" (defender) who is already there to support Alejandra. This implies that Helena likely preceded Alejandra in the author's spiritual or physical journey (as supported by blog entries dated 2019 for Helena and 2023 for Alejandra), creating a lineage of guidance within the family. The father positions Helena not just as a sister, but as a spiritual prototype who is "teaching" him what it means to love and sacrifice.

### 2. The Father's Spiritual Evolution through His Daughters
The author views his relationship with these daughters not just as a parenting experience, but as a path of personal enlightenment. In the blog post titled "Poco antes de Alejandra," he reflects that his daughter Alejandra is a **"regalo que tengo de la vida"** (a gift I have from life). However, the relationship is reciprocal: Helena teaches him about the **"negación del ego"** (negation of the ego) and the importance of defending others. By stating **"Y eso me lo ha enseñado mi hija Helena"** (And that was taught to me by my daughter Helena), the author reveals that the sisters' dynamic has shaped his own identity. They are the **"clave del faro"** (key of the lighthouse) for him, illuminating his understanding of existence, much like the light described in the March 2, 2024 post that "guides Alejandra."

### 3. The Metaphorical Duality of Light and Darkness
The father’s internal monologue often oscillates between the **consciousness** (mind) and the **physical world**. He asks, "How can I not be myself if I am living in the body, which is a decoration of the consciousness?" In this philosophical framework, the two sisters may represent two facets of this duality:
*   **Helena**, as the older or "first" companion, may represent the **established reality**, the tangible "matter" that has already arrived.
*   **Alejandra**, as the new arrival, represents the **future**, the continuing light, and the spiritual potential.

The text says, **"Luz que guie a Alejandra"** (Light that guides Alejandra). This suggests that Helena serves as that guiding light for her sister, bridging the gap between the author's spiritual teachings and their physical reality. The father observes that their relationship is not static but evolving, with Alejandra entering the narrative of his life as a new chapter of light.

### 4. The Father's Identity within the Family
The father describes himself as **"no el protagonista sino también el autor"** (not the protagonist but also the author). In the context of his family, he positions himself as the **narrator** whose story is being written through his daughters. The relationship with Helena and Alejandra is the content of his life's story. He does not claim credit for their existence but attributes it to destiny and love: **"La vida tiene un plan para cada uno"** (Life has a plan for each one). The "plan" involves him as the father who witnesses their journey, and them as the subjects of his affection. His identity is inextricably linked to theirs; they are the proof of his love and the medium through which his spiritual lessons (faith, defense of others, light) are enacted in the world.

### Conclusion
In essence, Helena and Alejandra are **sisters and daughters**, bound by a father who sees his life’s purpose reflected in their existence. Helena is the protector and teacher, guiding Alejandra who embodies the future light. For the author, their relationship is a living testament to his belief in a purposeful universe, where the **"ironía del universo"** is resolved through the love between father and children, and sister and sister. They are not just family; they are the **"compañeros"** (companions) on his spiritual walk, with Helena serving as the shield and light for the younger Alejandra, fulfilling the father's deepest hopes for their shared destiny.
